Prevent Fires Caused by Cooking

  • Consider staying in the kitchen while cooking and watching carefully when children are present. Teach them to know the drill.
  • Towels, food packaging and potholders can burn so make a point to keep these and other flammable items away from your stove top and oven.
  • There is less chance of an accident if you avoid cooking in loosely fitted clothing. Remember when cooking to roll up your sleeves.
  • Learn from the experts how to put out a small pan fire by turning off the burner and quickly sliding a lid over the flames, or smothering it with baking soda.
  • Unattended barbeque grills can be dangerous. Children need supervision any time they are around a burning grill.
  • Fire experts remind us to keep grills at least three feet away from the house, plants and other objects and avoid cooking under eaves or low hanging branches.
  • Consider having everyone in your home learn when and how to use a fire extinguisher
