How Fire Safe Is Your Home?

Preparation is Key

We all think our homes are safe. We often do activities to improve its condition and long-term value, but how fire safe is your home today? The purpose of this section is to help you identify and learn ways you can address that question. The best way to start is to prepare a checklist and have a plan.

Learn from the Fire Authorities

When you start your fire safety checklist, begin by learning what the fires safety authorities say first regarding fire safety and prevention. They will inform you about what to do and how to respond to a fire emergency. This should be considered a crucial skill for your family and a primary part of your family fire checklist. The way to begin is to study the options for your fire safety checklist, and involve your whole family to really be prepared.

Establish a Fire Safety Checklist

Our fire safety tips will point you to informative links and articles with checklists already established by recognized authorities. This information can provide you with insight into preventative home safety measures that you and your loved ones can practice as a part of your normal daily routines.

We have identified some of the best fire safety and prevention information available online from government and educational organizations. These sites can offer the methods, tools and techniques necessary to educate your family on fire prevention, maintenance and safety for your home.

Reduce the Risks

This section will also direct you to information to help describe ways you can prevent or reduce the risk of fire to make your home safer for your whole family. A family fire checklist itemizes preventative actions to consider as in a periodic review of typical fire hazards. Understanding electrical systems, fire safety equipment and their availability and condition can start you on your way to better fire safety.

The sites we have selected will provide you and your family with knowledge and recommendations of how to prevent fires. Try to visit two or three of these sites, as each will offer additional ideas and items to consider as you build your own Family Fire Safety Checklist. Continuously add and update your family's fire safety checklist.

Involve the Whole Family

You can also download specific checklists that have been designed to make it easy and fun for the whole family to consider. Some of the sites we have selected have interactive questionnaires that you and your children or loved ones can use to learn how to prepare your home and family for better fire safety awareness and prevention.

Practice your fire safety checklist and make sure your entire family stays involved. Integrate the fire checklist with your family's fire safety plans. The more knowledge and awareness we can have regarding fire safety and prevention, the greater peace of mind we can have knowing that we have educated and prepared our families in the event a fire accident should occur.
